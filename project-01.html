Understanding Road Fatalities in Australia: A Data‑Driven Insight for Smarter Road Safety Decisions

By Salitha Dinushika — Data Analyst | Statistical Modeller | Dashboard Designer

Overview

Road safety remains a critical public health challenge in Australia. Despite investments in enforcement, infrastructure, and education, the national annual fatality trend across the last 10 years remains relatively stable. This project investigates why that may be the case and identifies the high‑impact groups that should be prioritised for targeted interventions.

Annual fatalities trend over the past 10 years

1. Problem Statement

Although multiple agencies run road safety programs, the persistent average number of annual fatalities suggests limited effectiveness of current strategies. This project focuses on uncovering patterns by state, demographic group, road environment, and temporal factors to identify high-contributing groups, enabling better policy prioritisation and more effective allocation of limited resources.

2. Finding the Right Dataset

The analysis uses official records from the National Road Safety Data Hub: datahub.roadsafety.gov.au. The repository contains over 35 years of fatality records; for this analysis, the most recent 10 years were selected to ensure relevance to the current environment.

Dataset link: Australian Road Deaths Database (Sept 2025)

3. Tools Used

The full workflow was completed using Microsoft Excel: data transformation, cleaning, pivot tables, slicers, and a responsive dashboard layout. This demonstrates strong end‑to‑end analysis capability using a widely accessible tool.

4. Data Cleaning & Preparation

Key preprocessing steps included handling missing values, filtering relevant years, grouping categorical variables, and creating derived columns for improved aggregation and visualization. Below is a concise summary:

  • Missing values: Labeled as "Unknown" and kept for analysis to avoid information loss.
  • Year filter: Selected the last 10 years from the dataset.
  • Category grouping: Consolidated granular values (e.g., exact speed limits) into logical ranges (0–29, 30–59, 60–89, 90-119, 120+ km/h).
  • Derived columns: Created helper columns for Months, Maximum Speed Limit Groups, Age Groups, Time-of-Day Groups, and Road User Groups to simplify visualization.

Example: Grouping data and generating a new column for time zones.

6. Data Visualization & Interactive Dashboard

The dashboard presents all KPIs interactively using graphs and charts, allowing users to explore trends and patterns effectively and to:

  • Select year ranges and one or multiple states
  • See KPIs update automatically
  • Use the "High‑Contributing Groups" selector to reveal which demographic or environmental groups contribute most to fatalities based on chosen KPIs

Dashboard preview:

Highest Risk Profiles Feature: A dedicated space in the dashboard titled "Highest Risk Profiles" allows users to analyse the impact of specific factors. It includes tick boxes for each KPI, and users can select any combination of KPIs to instantly view the top three contributing risk groups. This enables targeted decision‑making, helping authorities prioritize safety interventions on the most critical profiles.

Conclusion & Next Steps

Targeted interventions guided by data are more likely to reduce road fatalities than one‑size‑fits‑all programs. By using this dashboard, policymakers and program designers can quickly identify priority groups and tailor interventions with measurable outcomes.
